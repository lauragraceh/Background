Background And Literature Review

Discussions about the changing purpose of public art and the online life of public art are already well established in the field; however, many of these are insufficient regarding what I plan to research. For example, in “Collaborative Systems: Redefining Public Art” the author Sharon Daniel challenges preconceived notions about public art as time moves forward, specifically regarding social justice; however, it fails to mention redefining public art regarding moving forward technologically. An example of an academic text within the field that does mention this is the research study “A cybergeography of public art encounter: The case of rubber duck.” by Martin Zebracki. This study faces an inverse problem to Daniel’s in that it focuses heavily on public art in a digital space, but fails to question if this could redefine public art. For my own research I will choose to focus on the overlap of these two insufficiencies.
